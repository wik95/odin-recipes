Pierogi (Polish Dumplings)

Description

Pierogi (the word 'pierogi'is plural in Polish, the singular is one 'pieróg' - pronounced pye-ROOG) are the most recognizable Polish food abroad. They are half-circular dumplings usually made from noodle flour dough, and sometimes from pastry dough. Pierogi are stuffed in a similar fashion to many other types of dumplings, for example the Japanese gyoza or the Italian ravioli.

Ingredients:

Potato Filling:

  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • ½ cup chopped onion
  • 2 cups cold mashed potatoes
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon white pepper

Dough:

  • 1 (8 ounce) container sour cream
  • 3 large eggs
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
    • <1i>¼ teaspoon salt

Steps:

  1. Prepare the sauerkraut filling: Melt the butter in a skillet over medium heat. Add the onions and fry them until they become translucent, for about 5 minutes. Add the sauerkraut, keep stirring occasionally, for 5 more minutes. Season with salt and pepper, then place it on a plate to cool.
  2. Prepare the potato filling: Melt the butter in the same skillet over medium heat. Add the onions. Cook and stir the onions until translucent, for about 5 minutes. Then add the mashed potatoes, salt, and white pepper.
  3. Make the dough: Beat the sour cream and eggs in a large bowl until smooth. Sift the flour, baking powder, and salt into a separate bowl; combine the dry ingridients with sour cream mixture until everything comes together. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface until firm and smooth.
  4. Divide the dough in half, then roll out one half to about an 1/8-inch thickness. Cut into 3-inch circles using a biscuit cutter. Place a small spoonful of potato filling into the center of each circle. Moisten the edges with water, fold over, and press the edges together with a fork to seal. Repeat the steps with the rest of the dough and the sauerkraut filling.
  5. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Add pierogies in batches into the boiling water and cook until they float up to the top, for about 3 to 5 minutes.
